Today’s Key Insight

Global markets are breathing a sigh of relief as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East appear to ease, driving down oil prices and lifting US equities. However, South Korea is experiencing a severe, idiosyncratic market dislocation; despite record-breaking semiconductor exports, domestic equities are plunging amid a historically weak currency and tightening domestic credit conditions. This stark divergence suggests investors are heavily discounting Korea’s macro export success in favor of mounting internal financial vulnerabilities.

Market Overview

US Equities Wall Street posted solid gains, led by the tech-heavy Nasdaq, as signals of a swift US military exit from Iran cooled geopolitical anxieties. The resulting drop in crude oil prices provided a significant macro tailwind, allowing investors to pivot back toward growth assets and highly anticipated corporate catalysts, such as the prospective SpaceX IPO.

Korean Equities Conversely, South Korean markets endured a brutal capitulation, with both the KOSPI and KOSDAQ shedding over 4%. This massive sell-off occurred despite the country posting a record $80 billion in March exports, pointing to deep domestic distress. A structurally weak won—remaining above the critical 1,500 threshold—combined with aggressive regulatory tightening on multi-homeowner mortgages appears to be draining domestic liquidity and overshadowing the semiconductor boom.
