Today’s Key Insight

The sudden escalation of Middle Eastern conflict, marked by an Iranian strike on US troops in Saudi Arabia, has violently reawakened stagflationary fears across global markets. As WTI crude breaches the $100 threshold and safe-haven assets surge, investors are rapidly unwinding risk positions in favor of defense and energy plays. This geopolitical premium threatens to complicate central bank trajectories, forcing policymakers into a difficult corner between taming supply-driven inflation and supporting faltering economic growth.

Market Overview

Wall Street suffered a broad and aggressive sell-off, with tech-heavy indices bearing the brunt of the impact as investors fled risk assets. The combination of the VIX spiking above 30 and rising Treasury yields suggests markets are pricing in both immediate geopolitical uncertainty and the prolonged inflationary consequences of a renewed energy shock.

South Korean equities displayed relative resilience on the surface, with the KOSPI posting only modest declines and the KOSDAQ edging slightly higher, likely supported by localized defensive rotations. However, the underlying macroeconomic picture is darkening for Seoul; a weakening won breaching the historic 1,500 level against the dollar, combined with surging global oil prices, is already exacerbating domestic inflation and pushing local mortgage rates higher, which will likely squeeze consumer spending.
