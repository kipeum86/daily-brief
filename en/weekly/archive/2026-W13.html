Editor's View

A narrative layer for the numbers and headlines

This Week’s Core Theme

The global economy is grappling with the severe ramifications of the conflict in Iran, as confirmation emerged that up to 40% of Gulf energy infrastructure has been destroyed. This geopolitical shock has triggered a cascading supply chain crisis, disrupting everything from Qatari helium exports vital to the technology sector to naphtha supplies essential for heavy industry. Consequently, stagflationary pressures are mounting, prompting a sharp retreat from risk assets as investors reassess the durability of industrial margins and tech hardware production.

Market Review

Volatility surged this week, with the VIX climbing 15.94% to 31.05 as markets priced in the escalating Middle Eastern conflict. WTI crude advanced 1.34% to $99.64, nearing the critical $100 threshold. Equities suffered broad declines, led by technology and manufacturing-heavy indices. The Nasdaq fell 3.23%, while South Korea’s KOSPI plunged 5.92%, battered by its heavy reliance on imported energy and industrial inputs.

The safe-haven US dollar strengthened, pushing the USD/KRW exchange rate up 1.24% to a strained 1,508.36. Interestingly, traditional alternative assets failed to catch a haven bid; gold and Bitcoin retreated by 1.72% and 1.65% respectively, suggesting that broader liquidity concerns and a strong dollar overshadowed their hedging appeal.

Top Stories

  • Gulf Infrastructure Devastated: French intelligence confirmed that 30-40% of Gulf energy infrastructure has been destroyed. The conflict has halted Qatari helium output, posing an immediate threat to global semiconductor supply chains.
  • Industrial Fallout: The energy crisis reached South Korea’s shores, forcing Lotte Chemical to suspend operations at its Yeosu plant amid a severe “naphtha shock.”
  • Geopolitical Realignments: US President Trump scheduled a May visit to China for talks with President Xi, while Washington reportedly linked Ukrainian security guarantees to territorial concessions in the Donbas.
  • Tech Liability: In a landmark legal precedent, Meta and YouTube were found liable in a major social media addiction trial.

What to Watch Next Week

Markets will closely monitor the stabilization of Gulf energy exports and any further disruptions to tech supply chains, particularly global helium inventories. In South Korea, attention will turn to domestic policy responses as rising home loan rates—now at a multi-year high of 4.32%—and proposed livelihood support funds intersect with a weakening won and industrial slowdowns.
