This Week’s Core Theme

A palpable sense of caution permeated global markets this week, particularly impacting South Korean equities amidst domestic concerns over financial stability and asset valuations. This broader risk aversion also saw declines in key commodities and a rise in volatility, even as US markets showed relative resilience.

Market Review

South Korean markets experienced a notable downturn, with the KOSPI index falling by 5.55% and the KOSDAQ by 2.14%. This was accompanied by a weakening of the Korean Won against the US Dollar, which appreciated by 0.75%. In contrast, US equities showed relative resilience, with the S&P 500 and Nasdaq posting modest declines of 0.45% and 1.11% respectively. Commodity markets saw significant retreats, with Gold dropping 4.26% and WTI Oil 3.91%. The VIX, a measure of market volatility, edged up by 2.46%, reflecting increased investor unease. Bitcoin, however, registered a slight gain of 1.39%.

Top Stories

Globally, a landmark verdict regarding social media addiction garnered significant attention, signaling potential shifts in big tech regulation. Geopolitical tensions remained prominent, with reports of Russia’s anticipated spring offensive in Ukraine and Hungary filing espionage charges against a journalist. Domestically, a Korean newspaper highlighted concerns over “Private Credit” and the potential for an “AI Bubble” to pose systemic risks, a sentiment that may have contributed to the sharp sell-off in Seoul.

What to Watch Next Week

Next week, market participants will likely continue to monitor geopolitical developments, particularly in Eastern Europe, and the evolving regulatory landscape for technology firms. Any further commentary or data points related to financial stability, especially concerning asset valuations and private credit markets, will be closely scrutinised for their potential to influence investor sentiment.
