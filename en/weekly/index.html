Editor's View

A narrative layer for the numbers and headlines

This Week’s Core Theme

Geopolitical anxieties, particularly concerning the Middle East, intensified market risk aversion this week, with South Korea’s markets bearing the brunt. While global indices saw modest declines, a notable surge in volatility underscored investor unease, prompting a flight from riskier assets in certain regions.

Market Review

South Korea’s KOSPI index experienced a significant decline of 5.92%, making it a leading laggard, while the KOSDAQ also fell by 1.72%. Concurrently, the Korean Won weakened considerably against the US Dollar, with USD/KRW rising 1.14%. This regional pressure contrasted with more contained movements in major Western markets; the S&P 500 edged down 0.45% and the Nasdaq fell 1.11%. The CBOE Volatility Index (VIX) climbed 7.28%, reflecting heightened uncertainty. In commodities, both Gold and WTI Oil retreated, falling 2.56% and 2.12% respectively, despite the underlying geopolitical tensions. Bitcoin remained largely flat.

Top Stories

The potential for a prolonged Middle East conflict emerged as a key concern, with South Korea’s National Assembly Budget Office warning that an extended war could reduce the nation’s growth rate by up to 0.5 percentage points. This assessment coincided with reports of deepening energy shocks within Korea. Globally, headlines continued to track the Middle East conflict, alongside a defence cooperation deal between Ukraine and Saudi Arabia. Elsewhere, Huawei’s new AI chip reportedly found favour with major Chinese tech firms, while a unit of Tong Ren Tang pulled its Hong Kong IPO, signalling potential caution in the region’s listing boom.

What to Watch Next Week

Market participants will continue to monitor geopolitical developments, particularly in the Middle East, for any signs of de-escalation or further escalation. Central bank communications and upcoming economic data releases will also be scrutinised for indications of monetary policy direction and global growth prospects amidst persistent uncertainties.
