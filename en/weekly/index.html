Editor's View

A narrative layer for the numbers and headlines

This Week’s Core Theme

Global markets this week were defined by a stark divergence: a severe geopolitical energy shock met with surprising equity resilience. The US Navy’s seizure of an Iranian-flagged vessel near the Strait of Hormuz drove a sharp rally in crude prices. Yet investors largely looked past the disruption, buoyed by robust economic data in Asia and major corporate transitions in the US technology sector.

Market Review

Energy markets absorbed the brunt of the week’s geopolitical friction. WTI crude surged 12.58% to $94.40 a barrel, pulling the VIX volatility index up 7.04% to 18.71. Curiously, gold failed to catch a haven bid, retreating 2.40% to $4,740.90.

Despite the spike in oil and volatility, equities advanced. South Korea’s KOSPI was the global standout, advancing 4.58% to 6,475.63 after a surprise expansion in Q1 GDP pushed domestic government bond yields higher. Wall Street maintained a steady footing, with the Nasdaq climbing 1.50% and the S&P 500 edging up 0.55%. In currency markets, the USD/KRW pair remained virtually flat at 1,476.32, while Bitcoin rallied 5.43%.

Top Stories

In the Middle East, Tehran vowed a swift response to the US Navy’s vessel seizure, raising fears of a broader disruption to maritime trade. In Europe, the EU unblocked a €90 billion loan to Ukraine, signaling sustained financial backing.

Corporate leadership and the AI arms race dominated business headlines. Apple announced that insider John Ternus will succeed Tim Cook as CEO, with Cook transitioning to executive chairman. Meanwhile, Google debuted custom chips to challenge Nvidia’s dominance, a move mirrored in Korea as Naver executives met with Nvidia to expand their AI footprint.

In Seoul, outgoing Bank of Korea Governor Rhee Chang-yong delivered his farewell address, emphasizing the limits of using interest rates alone to manage exchange rates and urging structural reforms. Domestically, the petrochemical sector faces mounting pressure, cutting workforces amid a severe naphtha supply shortage.

What to Watch Next Week

Markets will closely monitor the Strait of Hormuz for any Iranian retaliation that could push crude prices higher and test the resilience of the current equity rally. Investors will also assess the strategic direction of Apple under its new chief executive and gauge how South Korea’s petrochemical industry navigates its ongoing supply-chain squeeze.
