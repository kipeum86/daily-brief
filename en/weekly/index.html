Editor's View

A narrative layer for the numbers and headlines

This Week’s Core Theme

A severe geopolitical energy shock dictated global market dynamics this week. As the conflict involving Iran escalated, reports emerged that up to 40% of Gulf energy infrastructure has been destroyed. Compounded by Tehran’s move to formalize a “toll booth” regime in the critical Strait of Hormuz, the crisis has triggered a global supply squeeze. The fallout is acutely visible in energy-dependent manufacturing hubs like South Korea, where industrial disruptions and currency depreciation are compounding broader macroeconomic anxieties.

Market Review

Equities retreated globally as risk aversion took hold. The tech-heavy Nasdaq fell 3.23%, while the S&P 500 shed 2.12%. However, the most severe damage was concentrated in South Korea, where the KOSPI plunged 5.92%, reflecting the outsized vulnerability of its industrial base to energy shocks.

Volatility surged, with the VIX climbing 15.94% to cross the 31 mark, signaling elevated market stress. In commodities, WTI crude oil advanced 2.91% to break the $100 threshold, settling at $101.18. The energy crisis and a flight to safety pushed the US dollar higher against emerging market currencies; the USD/KRW exchange rate climbed 1.24% to breach the psychologically significant 1,500 level. Elsewhere, Bitcoin edged up 1.39%, while gold unexpectedly slipped 1.07%.

Top Stories

Geopolitics & Energy: France confirmed a massive degradation of Gulf energy infrastructure, while the Philippines declared a national energy emergency. The supply constraints are already hitting Asian industry, evidenced by Lotte Chemical suspending operations at its Yeosu plant due to a severe “naphtha shock.”

Global Business: Meta and YouTube were found liable in a landmark US trial regarding social media addiction. In the aerospace sector, Airbus secured a major 101-plane order from China, dealing another blow to Boeing, while space stocks rallied on rumors of an imminent SpaceX IPO.

South Korean Economy: Domestic financial pressures are mounting. February home loan rates reached a 27-month high of 4.32%. In response to the tightening economic environment, the ruling party and government are actively considering selective livelihood support funds and property tax reforms.

What to Watch Next Week

  • Strait of Hormuz: Any enforcement of Iran’s new transit regime will likely dictate the next directional move for Brent and WTI crude.
  • Bank of Korea & FX Intervention: With the won trading above 1,500 to the dollar, markets will be on high alert for verbal or direct interventions from South Korean financial authorities.
  • Geopolitical Diplomacy: Investors will monitor the conditional EU-US trade deal and ongoing negotiations regarding US security guarantees for Ukraine.
