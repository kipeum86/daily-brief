Editor's View

A narrative layer for the numbers and headlines

This Week’s Core Theme

Geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, particularly concerning Iran, dominated global markets this week, triggering a broad retreat from risk assets and a notable surge in volatility. The failure of diplomatic efforts to de-escalate the situation sent ripples across equities, with South Korean markets experiencing a particularly sharp downturn as the Won weakened against the dollar.

Market Review

Global equities largely retreated as uncertainty mounted. The S&P 500 declined by 0.98%, while the Nasdaq Composite fell by 1.98%, reflecting a broader risk-off sentiment. Volatility, as measured by the VIX, surged over 10%, indicating heightened investor anxiety. South Korea’s KOSPI bore the brunt of the regional impact, plummeting 5.55%, with the KOSDAQ also down 2.14%. Concurrently, the Korean Won depreciated against the US dollar, with USD/KRW rising 1.17% to 1,507.28. In commodities, WTI oil saw a modest decline of 0.79%, while gold remained largely flat. Cryptocurrencies also faced headwinds, with Bitcoin falling 2.15%. US 10-year Treasury yields edged higher, suggesting some flight to safety in US government bonds.

Top Stories

The primary driver of market sentiment was the news that President Trump’s extension efforts regarding Iran failed to soothe markets, leading the Dow to briefly enter correction territory. This geopolitical instability prompted South Korean Governor Kim Jin-tae to order an expanded response to the Middle East situation, activating emergency “Livelihood Stabilization Measures.” Domestically in the US, a positive development saw the Senate pass a bill to fund the TSA, promising an end to airport chaos. Meanwhile, discussions emerged regarding alternative oil pipelines in Saudi Arabia, UAE, and Iraq, highlighting ongoing concerns about energy security amidst regional tensions.

What to Watch Next Week

Investors will closely monitor upcoming US jobs data for further insights into the economic outlook, particularly as markets remain gripped by geopolitical concerns. Developments in the Middle East, especially any further diplomatic or military actions, will continue to be a critical determinant of market direction. Locally, the potential for a general strike at Samsung Electronics in May will be a key watch point for South Korean markets.
