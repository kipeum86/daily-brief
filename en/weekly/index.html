Editor's View

A narrative layer for the numbers and headlines

This Week’s Core Theme

A striking divergence defined global markets this week: escalating geopolitical risks sent energy and safe-haven assets soaring, yet US equities remained insulated by relentless tech optimism. While threats to Middle Eastern energy infrastructure pushed crude oil to extremes, Wall Street looked past the geopolitical friction, anchored by record-breaking artificial intelligence valuations and robust semiconductor output.

Market Review

Commodity markets absorbed the brunt of this week’s geopolitical shocks. WTI crude surged 12.46% to $112.06 a barrel, tracking Brent’s spike past $116 following US threats against Iranian export hubs. Gold advanced 4.69% to $4,702.70, reflecting sustained demand for safe-haven assets.

Despite the energy shock, US equities rallied forcefully. The Nasdaq climbed 4.44% and the S&P 500 added 3.36%. Consequently, the VIX volatility index retreated 23.12% to 23.87, and US 10-year Treasury yields slipped 2.86%, suggesting investors are currently discounting the risk of a broader macro contagion.

South Korean markets, however, decoupled from the US rally. The tech-heavy KOSDAQ tumbled 6.81% and the KOSPI shed 1.13%. The won remained under pressure, with the USD/KRW exchange rate edging up to 1,510.54, reflecting capital outflows and currency weakness despite strong domestic industrial data.

Top Stories

Geopolitics & Energy: Oil markets were jolted by US threats to target Iranian oil wells, overshadowing other geopolitical developments, including the docking of a Russian tanker in Cuba and the lifting of US sanctions on Venezuela’s acting president.

Tech & Corporate: OpenAI closed a record-breaking $122 billion funding round, accelerating anticipation for a landmark IPO. In traditional sectors, Sysco announced a $29 billion buyout to expand its food-service footprint, while the US administration threatened 100% tariffs on drugmakers failing to cut prices or onshore production.

South Korea: Domestic semiconductor production jumped 28%, marking the largest increase in 38 years. Concurrently, the government signaled an expansionary fiscal stance, projecting a budget approaching 800 trillion won for next year.

What to Watch Next Week

The Bank of Korea’s impending interest rate decision will be the focal point for domestic markets, particularly as policymakers navigate a weak won and diverging local equity performance. Globally, investors will monitor whether the sustained spike in crude prices begins to bleed into broader inflation metrics, potentially testing the resilience of the current tech-led equity rally.
